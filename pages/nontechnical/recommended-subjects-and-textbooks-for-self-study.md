(Updated 23 Feb 2023) Noncomprehensive list of textbooks and resources that I like.


#### Linear algebra

- [Linear Algebra Done Right, by Sheldon Axler](https://linear.axler.net/)
  - [Down with Determinants](https://www.axler.net/DwD.html)
  - [But also we need determinants](../math/levi-civita-determinants-volumes-and-jacobians.md)
- [An Introduction to Tensors and Group Theory for Physicists, by Nadir Jeevanjee](https://www.amazon.com/Introduction-Tensors-Group-Theory-Physicists/dp/3319147935)
- [Richard E. Borcherds YouTube lectures](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCIyDqfi_cbkp-RU20aBF-MQ)
- [Frederic Schuller YouTube lectures](https://www.youtube.com/@FredericSchuller)
- [Applied Analysis, by Hunter and Nachtergaele](https://www.amazon.com/Applied-Analysis-John-K-Hunter/dp/9812705430)
- [Bright side of mathematics](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCdwo4k1RQHTcq_-WS7Cazqg)
- [Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach, by Arora and Barak](http://theory.cs.princeton.edu/complexity/)
- [Geometry, Topology and Physics, by Mikio Nakahara](https://www.amazon.com/Geometry-Topology-Physics-Graduate-Student/dp/0750306068)
- [Algebraic topology, by Allen Hatcher](https://pi.math.cornell.edu/~hatcher/AT/AT.pdf)
- [Quantum Continuous Variables: A Primer of Theoretical Methods, by Alessio Serafini](https://www.amazon.com/Quantum-Continuous-Variables-Theoretical-Methods/dp/1482246341)
