{% include post-header.md %}

(13 Sep 2021) Hausdorff topological manifolds are often required to be paracompact so that a partition of unity can be defined. In this post, I will write down my intuition for why paracompactness is needed in order to define a partition of unity on a manifold. I will not work through the [proof that paracompact Hausdorff topological spaces admit a partition of unity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paracompact_space#Proof_that_paracompact_Hausdorff_spaces_admit_partitions_of_unity), but rather write down some vague intuition to at least understand where the motivation to define paracompactness comes from.
